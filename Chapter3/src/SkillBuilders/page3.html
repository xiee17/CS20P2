Lanzhou beef noodles--My favorite cuisine

hictorical introuction

Lanzhou beef noodles, also known as Lanzhou hand-pulled noodles, originated in Lanzhou, Gansu Province, China, during the early Qing Dynasty. The dish was first popularized by Hui Muslims, who brought their culinary traditions to the region. It is celebrated for its "one clear, two white, three red, four green, and five yellow" characteristics—referring to the clear broth, white radish slices, red chili oil, green cilantro, and yellow noodles. The art of making Lanzhou noodles involves an intricate hand-pulling technique that creates long, springy strands of dough. Over time, Lanzhou beef noodles became a cultural symbol of the city and gained national fame for their unique flavor and craftsmanship, representing the rich culinary heritage of northwest China.

Ingredient

Beef: Usually brisket or shank, for a rich and flavorful broth. Bone: Beef or lamb bones for making the soup base. Noodles: Hand-pulled wheat noodles with a chewy texture. Daikon Radish: Thinly sliced, providing a sweet and refreshing balance. Spices: A blend of star anise, cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, Sichuan peppercorns, and bay leaves. Aromatics: Garlic, ginger, green onions. Chili Oil: Adds a spicy and aromatic layer. Soy Sauce: Light soy sauce for seasoning. Salt: To taste, for the broth. Cilantro and Green Onion: Chopped for garnish, adding freshness.
